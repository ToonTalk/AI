The Story of One Extraordinary Night

The Royal Ball

From the pumpkin carriage to the glass staircase — everything that happened the evening my life changed forever.

Chapter I

The Fairy Godmother

I had not been permitted to attend the ball. My stepmother presented my stepsisters in their finest gowns and left me alone in the kitchen, weeping beside the cold hearth.

Then — a shimmer in the air. A figure, small and warm and utterly impossible, appeared before me. She called herself my fairy godmother, and she spoke as if we had always known each other.

"Dry your tears, my dear. You shall go to the ball."

"She waved her wand and the pumpkin became the most magnificent carriage I have ever seen — pearl-white, gilded in gold."

The mice became horses. The lizards became footmen. My rags became a gown of midnight blue silk, threaded through with silver, as though someone had sewn a piece of the sky.

Chapter II

Arriving at the Palace

The carriage swept through the palace gates. The footmen handed me down as though I were genuine royalty — and in that moment, I felt as though I truly was.

Every head on the grand staircase turned. Candlelight caught the silver thread in my gown and threw tiny stars across the ceiling. I had to remind myself to breathe.

"At the top of the stairs, a young man in deep blue stood apart from the crowd — and he was looking only at me."

The herald announced no name for me — I had not given one — but the prince descended the stairs without a moment's hesitation and offered his hand.

· · · ✦ · · ·
Chapter III

The Dance

I do not know how long we danced. Time seemed to pool and slow. The orchestra played waltzes that felt written for us alone, and the crowd fell back to watch.

He asked my name. I told him only Ella. He said that was the most beautiful name he had ever heard, and I laughed — truly laughed — for the first time in years.

We talked between dances on the moonlit balcony. He spoke of loneliness inside a palace full of people. I understood that better than he could have imagined.

"For a few hours, standing beside him, I forgot entirely that I did not belong."

Evening by the Clock

6:00 pm

Stepsisters depart for the palace

I was left alone with a mountain of mending and a cold hearth.

6:30 pm

The fairy godmother appears

A pumpkin, six mice, two lizards, and a few kind words — and the whole evening transformed.

8:00 pm

Arrival at the Royal Palace

The carriage swept through the palace gates. Every head turned on the grand staircase.

8:15 pm

First dance with the prince

He descended the stairs without a word to anyone else and offered me his hand.

10:30 pm

Moonlit balcony conversation

We talked for what felt like minutes and must have been an hour. He was nothing like I expected a prince to be.

11:58 pm

The clock begins to strike

I heard the first chime and my heart dropped. I pulled free and ran.

12:00 am

One slipper on the palace steps

I reached the carriage just as it began to shrink. The slipper — I could not go back for it.
