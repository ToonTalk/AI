Ask Fairy G

Magical Advice & Enchanted Etiquette for the Modern Princess

Welcome to my weekly column! Whether you're struggling with wand-waving techniques, rodent management, or dealing with difficult step-families, I'm here to sprinkle a little clarity (and a lot of glitter) on your most pressing royal dilemmas. Bibbidi-Bobbidi-Boo!

Curfew Rules: Why 12:01 is Too Late

"Dear Fairy G, I'm having the best time at the ball. The Prince is charming, the music is perfect. Can't I just stay until 12:15? What's the worst that could happen?"
— Chronologically Challenged in the Courtyard

Dear Challenged,

I cannot stress this enough: When the clock strikes twelve, you must run! Transfiguration spells are highly unstable after a 4-hour cycle. The exact moment the final chime finishes, the magical binding collapses.

If you wait until 12:01, you won't just be embarrassed; you will be sitting in a squashed gourd in the middle of the dance floor, wearing rags, surrounded by bewildered mice. Your elegant footmen will revert to rodents while carrying your train. It’s a logistical nightmare and an absolute faux pas.

A true lady always leaves them wanting more. Flee at the first stroke of midnight. Trust me.

With sparkling regards,
Fairy G

Pumpkins: Not Just for Pie Anymore

"Dear Fairy G, I requested a carriage for my grand entrance, but you sent me to the vegetable patch. Are you sure a pumpkin is structurally sound for highway travel?"
— Skeptical about Squash

Dear Skeptical,

Ah, the classic pumpkin misconception! Pumpkins are actually the premier choice for enchanted transport. They possess an incredibly sturdy natural ribbing that, when enlarged to 300x its normal size and gilded with a silver-spindle charm, rivals the suspension of any royal coach.

Watermelons are too leaky, and zucchinis lack the necessary headroom for a proper tiara. Just remember: no sudden stops, and try to avoid the royal chef's carving knives if you park near the kitchens!

Always practical,
Fairy G

Glass Slippers: Blister Prevention 101

"Dear Fairy G, Glass is rigid, non-breathable, and famously fragile. How am I supposed to waltz in these things without needing a royal physician on standby?"
— Hesitant Heel-Wearer

Dear Hesitant,

A valid concern! However, enchanted glass is not your average windowpane. These slippers are forged from crystallized starlight and spun sugar-glass. They dynamically mold to your exact foot shape upon entry, providing orthopedically perfect arch support.

Furthermore, they are lined with an invisible anti-friction charm. You won't get a single blister! Just be aware: the custom fit is so precise that if anyone else tries to wear them, they simply won't fit. Great for securing your identity; bad for sharing with your sisters.

Treading lightly,
Fairy G

Mice to Men: Footmen Etiquette

"Dear Fairy G, My footmen keep trying to eat the cheese from the hors d'oeuvres tray and hiding in the wainscoting. Help!"
— Frazzled by Fauna

Dear Frazzled,

You can take the mouse out of the cinders, but you can't entirely take the cinders out of the mouse! When animals are transfigured into human staff, they retain a few... quirks.

My top tip: Before the ball, feed your horses (mice), coachman (rat), and footmen (lizards) a hearty, species-appropriate meal. A full footman is much less likely to snap his tongue at a passing fly during the royal procession. And maybe keep them away from the King's prized feline, Lucifer.

Yours in transformation,
Fairy G
