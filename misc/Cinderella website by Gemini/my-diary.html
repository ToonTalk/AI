Secret Thoughts

Late evening, by the dying embers...

Dearest Diary,

My hands are so raw tonight, and my knees ache terribly. I must have scrubbed the great hall's stone floor three times over. Anastasia and Drizella spilled their tea on purpose just to watch me clean it up again. They laughed so cruelly, twirling in their new velvet dresses and boasting about the upcoming Royal Ball. I tried to smile and look down, but my heart felt as heavy as the iron pots I polished all afternoon.

Stepmother says I am just a ragged cinder-girl, meant only for ashes and endless chores. But when I am finally alone in the dark, I do not see the dusty hearth. I hear the faintest, most beautiful waltz playing in the distance. I dream of a life where I am kind, where I am loved, and where I can dance under a canopy of stars instead of sweeping the chimney soot.

I must keep these dreams locked away here on these pages, for they are the only things I truly own. No matter how many floors I scrub, or how cold the cinders grow, they cannot wash away my hope. I will keep believing that, someday, my life will change, and the magic I feel in my heart will finally become real.

With a hopeful heart,
Cinderella
