Happily Ever After

The Morning After the Ball

Dear Diary, I can scarcely believe my own words as I write them. My hands are still trembling with a profound, radiant joy I never truly dared to dream of. Just hours ago, the Grand Duke arrived at our estate, bearing a velvet pillow and the very glass slipper I had left behind on the palace steps.

My stepmother and stepsisters tried desperately to force their feet into the delicate, glowing glass. They pushed and wept, but of course, it was entirely to no avail. The magic remembers; it knows to whom it belongs.

✦ ✧ ✦

When I finally stepped forward from the shadows of the hall, the room went entirely still. The Grand Duke's royal footman knelt before me and gently slid the slipper onto my foot. Oh, Diary... it fit perfectly. A flawless, warm match that seemed to illuminate the dark foyer. My stepmother's face drained of all color, and a gasp echoed through the room when I reached into the pocket of my humble apron and quietly produced its sparkling twin.

The spell wasn't just a fleeting dream for one night. The magic is real, and it is mine. As I sit here upon my little bed in the attic for the very last time, my meager belongings are packed into a single, humble bag. I don't need to take much; everything I could ever want is waiting for me. Through the dusty window, I can see the royal carriage waiting downstairs in the courtyard.

I am leaving this cold room, the cinders, and the cruelty behind forever. I am going to the palace. I am going to him. And so, my dearest Diary, this must be my final entry. My story in these ashes is done, and my beautiful new life begins today.

Forever yours,
Cinderella
