Collision time calculation: The trains must close a gap of 100 miles at a combined rate of 100 mph. Time = Distance √∑ Speed = 100 miles √∑ 100 mph = 1 hour.
Fly distance calculation: The fly maintains constant speed of 75 mph for the entire duration until collision. The fly is in continuous motion for exactly 1 hour. Distance = Speed √ó Time = 75 mph √ó 1 hour = 75 miles.

The Two-Train Fly Problem: Complete Solutions

The Easy Solution

Problem Setup: Two trains start 100 miles apart, each traveling toward the other at 50 mph. A fly starts on one train and flies back and forth at 75 mph until the trains collide.
Calculation:
• Closing speed of trains = 50 mph + 50 mph = 100 mph

• Time until collision = 100 miles √∑ 100 mph = 1 hour

• Fly's distance = 75 mph √ó 1 hour = 75 miles
The insight: Instead of tracking the fly's complex zigzag path, recognize that the fly flies continuously at constant speed for exactly as long as it takes the trains to meet. Since we can easily calculate when the trains collide (1 hour), we immediately know the fly's total distance.

The Hard Solution (Infinite Series)

Leg 1: Fly starts at Train A heading toward Train B.
• Approach speed: 75 mph (fly) + 50 mph (Train B) = 125 mph

• Initial distance: 100 miles

• Time: 100 √∑ 125 = 0.8 hours

• Fly travels: 75 √ó 0.8 = 60 miles

• Trains advance: 50 √ó 0.8 = 40 miles each

• Remaining separation: 100 - 40 - 40 = 20 miles
Leg 2: Fly reverses at Train B, heads back toward Train A.
• Approach speed: 125 mph

• Distance: 20 miles

• Time: 20 √∑ 125 = 0.16 hours

• Fly travels: 75 √ó 0.16 = 12 miles

• Trains advance: 50 √ó 0.16 = 8 miles each

• Remaining separation: 20 - 8 - 8 = 4 miles
Leg 3: Fly heads back toward Train B.
• Distance: 4 miles

• Time: 4 √∑ 125 = 0.032 hours

• Fly travels: 75 √ó 0.032 = 2.4 miles

• Trains advance: 50 √ó 0.032 = 1.6 miles each

• Remaining separation: 4 - 1.6 - 1.6 = 0.8 miles
Leg 4: Fly returns toward Train A.
• Distance: 0.8 miles

• Time: 0.8 √∑ 125 = 0.0064 hours

• Fly travels: 75 √ó 0.0064 = 0.48 miles

• Remaining separation: 0.16 miles
Pattern Recognition: The sequence of distances is 60, 12, 2.4, 0.48, ...
Each term is 1/5 of the previous term. This is a geometric series with:
• First term: a = 60

• Common ratio: r = 1/5 = 0.2
Why r = 1/5? During each leg taking time t, the trains close the gap by 100t (combined speed). The fly covers distance 75t while the gap shrinks from d to d - 100t. Setting d - 100t for the next leg in terms of the original d: if the fly takes time d/125 to cross distance d, then the new gap is d - 100(d/125) = d - 0.8d = 0.2d. Therefore each gap is 1/5 of the previous gap, and since fly speed is constant, each distance traveled is also 1/5 of the previous distance.
Series Sum: Using the geometric series formula S = a/(1-r):
S = 60/(1 - 0.2) = 60/0.8 = 75 miles
Alternatively: 60(1 + 0.2 + 0.04 + 0.008 + ...) = 60 √ó 1.25 = 75 miles

Verification

Both methods yield 75 miles, confirming the answer. The easy solution uses the fact that total flight time equals total collision time (1 hour). The hard solution explicitly sums the infinite series of individual leg distances, demonstrating that 60 + 12 + 2.4 + 0.48 + ... converges to the same result through the geometric series formula.
