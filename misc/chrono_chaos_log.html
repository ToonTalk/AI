Anomaly #1
Target Era: 79 AD, Pompeii
Observation: A young woman carefully places a freshly laundered tunic on a stone wall to dry under the morning sun, adjusting it to catch the gentle breeze.
Traveler Intervention:
"As soon as she leaves you pull out your squirt gun and soak the tunic."
Timeline Re-designated: The Soaked Tunic Schism
Immediate Effect The young woman, a priestess named Livia, intended that tunic for a crucial pre-eruption appeasement ritual to Vulcan, scheduled for the very morning. Discovering her sacred garment soaked, she was forced to substitute it with a common, ill-fitting cloth, an act widely interpreted as an immediate and dire omen of disrespect before Vesuvius erupted hours later.
+100 Years A century later, the volcanic region is consumed by an enduring spiritual crisis. The 'Tunic Blasphemy' is enshrined in local lore as the definitive reason for Vulcan's wrath and Pompeii's destruction. This leads to a widespread rejection of traditional Roman pantheon worship in Southern Italy, fostering a vacuum filled by zealous, often violent, proto-Christian sects and a surge in esoteric, doomsday cults predicting endless cycles of fiery retribution, creating constant regional instability.
Year 3000 By the year 3000, the world is a fractured mosaic of technocratic city-states and neo-pagan empires, none achieving global dominance. The 'Tunic Incident' is now a central tenet of the 'Cult of the Immaculate Garment,' which believes that specific fabric moisture levels dictate cosmic harmony. Humanity lives under the constant threat of 'Divine Desiccation Events' (catastrophic droughts or floods) if sacred laundromats fail to precisely balance dampness. Megastructures called 'Vesuvian Vents' perpetually release steam into the atmosphere, a misguided attempt to appease the perpetually 'offended' Vulcan, whose digital avatar now dictates global climate policy based on humidity algorithms.
