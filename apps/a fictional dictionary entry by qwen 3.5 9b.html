Zephyr-Bloom

[zif-er-bloom]
noun (botany)

A rare, bioluminescent flora found exclusively in the lower atmosphere of Gas-Giant-X, characterized by its ability to absorb neon radiation and convert it into a soft, violet luminescence.

verb (intransitive)
"As the alien sun began to set, the valley floor was suddenly illuminated by the zephyr-blooms, creating an ethereal aurora that danced with the passing wind."
Early 2042 Coined by Dr. Aris Thorne From the prefix zephyr- (referring to the specific zonal wind current) + -bloom (to flower/open).
Revised 2067 Merriam-Webster Galactic Supplement Usage expanded to include terrestrial usage due to climate change altering atmospheric patterns.
