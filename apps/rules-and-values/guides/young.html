How to play

You're in charge of the playground. You write the rules. The computer shows you who your rule helps — and who it hurts. Then you make it better.

What you do

1

Write a rule. Type it in your own words, like “Everyone can play however they want.” You can press the 🎤 mic and just say it out loud instead.

2

Press “Test it.” The computer looks at your rule and finds one kid it affects.

3

Meet that kid. Read what happened to them. Press 🔊 to hear it read out loud. Look at Your playground on the left to see who's helped and who's hurt now.

4

Don't like it? Fix it. Press Revise a rule, change your words, and test again. Watch the kids change color.

5

Name the pattern. When fixing one thing breaks another thing, you get to give it a name in your own words. It goes in your Field guide — that's your list of playground wisdom.

What the colors mean

Green = your rule helps this kid right now Red = your rule hurts this kid right now Grey = your rule doesn't touch them

Here's the tricky part: a rule that turns one kid green often turns another kid red. That's normal! Every rule is like that.

If the computer read your rule wrong

Sometimes the computer reads your rule in a way you didn't mean. If that happens, press “That's not a fair reading” and explain — quote your own words. If you're right, the computer will say “okay, you win” and you keep your rule. Catching a confusing rule is a win, not a mistake!

⭐ The big idea: There is no perfect rule. You don't win by making a rule with zero problems — nobody can. You win by choosing which problems you can live with, and knowing why.
🎤 Talk instead of type. Turn on 🔊 speech at the top. Then a mic button shows up — press it and say your rule out loud. Press 🔊 on any scene to hear it read to you.
