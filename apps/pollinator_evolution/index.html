Pollinator Predator Garden Evolution

Choose a version to play and read about the changes from the previous version:

  • V01 – Base version: bees pollinate flowers, flowers reproduce, spiders hunt bees.
  • V02 – Adds nectar to flowers; bees prefer flowers with nectar and consume it.
  • V03 – Bees remember their last flower and avoid returning to it immediately.
  • V04 – Nectar regenerates faster on flowers.
  • V05 – Introduces a hive: bees occasionally return home and spawn new bees.
  • V06 – Day/night cycle: bees rest at night and spiders become more active.
  • V07 – Two flower types with distinct colors and nectar regeneration rates.
  • V08 – Bees leave pollen trails that fade over time.
  • V09 – Spiders can drop webs that temporarily trap bees.
  • V10 – Weather system: rain slows agents and boosts nectar regeneration.
  • V11 – Birds now roam the garden, hunting spiders and keeping their numbers in check.
  • V12 – Flowers gain genetic traits: each bloom has its own nectar regeneration rate and colour, which offspring inherit with mutations.
  • V13 – A queen bee at the hive boosts the speed of nearby bees with her pheromones.
  • V14 – Press V to cycle viewpoints, highlighting bees, spiders, flowers or birds.
  • V15 – Flowers mutate in size, resulting in blooms of varying dimensions.
  • V16 – Bees now avoid crowded flowers, balancing pollinator attention across the garden.
  • V17 – Environmental zones: fertile areas boost nectar, dry areas slow growth, and a toxic zone at the top harms agents.
  • V18 – Use special powers: press F to freeze spiders, P to fertilise all flowers, and B to scatter bees.
  • V19 – A live ecosystem balance meter displays counts of bees, flowers, spiders and birds.
  • V20 – Maintain healthy populations to achieve a balanced ecosystem and unlock a congratulatory message.
  • V21 – Balanced improvements: refined predator–prey dynamics, population caps, progress meter, zone legend, restart button, control legend, trail limits, and a simple population trend graph.
