First Words

A ship is coming. The minds aboard know only a handful of human words. You can only reach them with words they already hold — or by teaching a new word, in words they have. Every word you give them, they keep.

They are listening.

The ship is 5 days out, and closing. Its minds are curious and mean no harm — but human speech is hard for them. They start out knowing the commonest words and nothing rarer. Send them a message. If you lean on a word they don't have, they'll say so. To hand them a new word, write it and explain it in (round brackets), using only words they already hold. Once they've got it, it's theirs — just say it plainly next time.

And why you? You are not on Earth — you are aboard a far station, the nearest human craft to the ship's path. Earth is too distant for a quick back-and-forth, but from here the lag is short enough to truly talk. So the task is yours: reach the ship, learn what you can, and relay each answer home while there is still time to ready a welcome.

Earth opened the channel first. Here is as far as that slow, badly-lagged exchange got — the last of it on record, before the talking became yours:

Earth's first words — and a word taught
Earth
Why do you travel (go far, for many days) to us?
🛸 the ship
we travel to see you. to learn. travel — known now.
the next turn — they kept it, one more taught
Earth
How far did you travel? Are you near (close now, not far) us?
🛸 the ship
far. past many stars. near — known. yes. near now.

Then the lag grew too long for Earth to keep up. You are nearer — take the channel from here. Everything they learned, they still hold.
