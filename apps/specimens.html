What is happening here?

Each specimen is a drawing made by a tiny program, shown below the picture. A turtle holds a pen and follows the instructions — forward, turn right, turn left, pen up, pen down. The program runs twelve times over.

When you pick a specimen you like, six children are born from it. Each child has the same program as the parent, but with small random changes. Some children look almost the same. Some look quite different. A few might surprise you.

This is how evolution works. Things that are chosen leave descendants. Tiny changes accumulate. Over many generations, beautiful and strange forms emerge — not because anyone designed them, but because of which ones got picked.
